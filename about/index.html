Company

Northwatch Systems builds the economic infrastructure for AI operations.

AI spend is becoming a material operating line item, but most teams still investigate it with billing exports, traces, and guesswork. Northwatch exists to make that spend explainable, attributable, and defensible.

Why we exist

Cloud spend got FinOps. AI spend needs runtime evidence.

One user action can fan out through prompts, retries, tools, fallbacks, agents, and model routes before it becomes a line on an invoice. Tags and dashboards can show pieces of that path; they rarely prove what changed, who owns it, or what the fix is worth.

Blackridge is the first Northwatch platform. It traces AI spend back to workflows, teams, decisions, and evidence rows so finance and engineering can investigate the same facts.

How we build

Design-partner first, narrow by default.

Blackridge is built with teams running real production AI workflows, not with synthetic demos alone. We start small, prove evidence quality, and expand only when the cause file survives both finance and engineering review.

01

One workflow first.

Scope starts around a single expensive or uncertain AI workflow so deployment risk and review effort stay contained.

02

Evidence before claims.

Observed spend, modeled opportunity, confidence, and coverage stay separate. If Blackridge cannot prove a claim, it says so.

03

Technical buyers in the room.

Architecture, security posture, fail-open behavior, and reproducibility are part of the first conversation, not late-stage paperwork.

What we are building toward

The accountability layer for AI operations.

Blackridge starts with AI Runtime Economics: attribution, forensics, workflow reconstruction, and finance-ready evidence. The larger Northwatch thesis is simple: as AI moves deeper into production, every runtime decision that spends money, changes risk, or affects customers will need an accountable record.
