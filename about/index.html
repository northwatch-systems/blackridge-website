Company

Northwatch Systems builds the economic infrastructure for AI operations.

We spent our careers inside large-scale platform engineering — the kind of environments where 33 million users depend on your systems, regulators in 17 jurisdictions audit your decisions, and a memory regression is measured in real money.

We've architected ground-up rewrites of revenue-critical monoliths, run Kubernetes fleets under GitOps, and built evaluation engines that process tens of millions of telemetry rows without breaking a sweat.

Then AI spend arrived, and we watched disciplined engineering organizations lose the ability to answer a basic question: what did we get for that money? Cloud cost had FinOps. AI cost had a shrug.

Blackridge — our flagship platform — is the first Northwatch product: AI Runtime Economics, delivered as attribution, forensics, reconstruction, and evidence. It won't be the last. The runtime layer of AI is accumulating the same unexplained line items cloud spend did a decade ago, and we intend to build the accountability layer for all of them.
