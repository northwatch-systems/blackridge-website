# assessment_strategy.md

## Purpose

The assessment page exists to create conversation.

The homepage established the problem.

The architecture page established technical confidence.

The assessment page exists to help visitors decide whether spending time with Blackridge is worthwhile.

The goal is not to generate the maximum number of leads.

The goal is to generate high-quality conversations with organizations experiencing real Runtime Economics challenges.

---

## Visitor

Primary visitors:

* CTOs
* VP Engineering
* Platform Leaders
* Infrastructure Leaders
* Technical Founders
* FinOps Stakeholders

Secondary visitors:

* Staff Engineers
* Principal Engineers
* Architects evaluating solutions on behalf of leadership

---

## Visitor State On Arrival

The visitor likely already believes:

* AI inference costs will continue to grow.
* Visibility gaps probably exist.
* Blackridge understands the problem.
* The architecture appears credible.

The visitor is now asking:

* What happens if I talk to these people?
* What do I get from the assessment?
* Is this worth my time?
* Am I entering a sales funnel?
* Will this conversation be useful even if I do not buy anything?

---

## Visitor State On Exit

The visitor should leave believing:

1. The assessment provides value.
2. The conversation is worth their time.
3. Blackridge understands infrastructure realities.
4. The founders will provide practical feedback.
5. They are not being pushed into a sales process.
6. Requesting an assessment is the logical next step.

---

## What This Page Must Explain

* What the assessment is
* What the visitor receives
* Who the assessment is for
* What happens during the process
* Why Blackridge is interested in this problem
* How to begin the conversation

---

## What This Page Must NOT Explain

* Detailed architecture
* Runtime topology
* Request lifecycle
* Technical implementation
* Future roadmap
* Governance concepts
* Product feature lists

The visitor already has access to the architecture page if they want technical depth.

---

## Core Narrative

The assessment page tells a simple story:

Most organizations do not fully understand the operational shape of their AI usage.

The Runtime Economics Assessment exists to identify visibility gaps, attribution gaps, optimization opportunities, and operational risks.

The assessment is founder-led, practical, and designed to provide useful insight regardless of whether Blackridge is ultimately the right solution.

---

## Trust Signals

The page should create trust through:

* Transparency
* Practicality
* Low-pressure language
* Clear expectations
* Demonstrated understanding of operational realities

The page should not rely on:

* Founder names
* Scarcity tactics
* Aggressive sales language
* Marketing claims
* Urgency pressure

---

## Assessment Philosophy

The assessment is not a demo.

The assessment is not a sales presentation.

The assessment is a technical and operational review.

The visitor should feel:

> "These people are trying to understand my environment."

not:

> "These people are trying to close a deal."

---

## Conversion Philosophy

Visitors should be able to engage immediately.

Visitors should not be forced to consume large amounts of content before acting.

The page should support two modes:

### Ready To Talk

The visitor already wants a conversation.

They should be able to request an assessment within seconds.

### Still Evaluating

The visitor wants additional context.

They should be able to learn:

* what they will receive
* who the assessment helps
* what the process looks like

before deciding.

Both paths should feel natural.

## Success Metric

A visitor finishes the assessment page and thinks:
