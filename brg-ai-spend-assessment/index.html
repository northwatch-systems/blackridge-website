Scope and boundaries

What the assessment does—and what it does not claim.

The assessment reconstructs the evidence available for one workflow, separates observed behavior from modeled opportunity, and makes limitations visible rather than hiding them.

Included in the assessment
Reconstruct spend for one expensive workflow from available logs, traces, runtime events, or imported records.
Attribute costs by app, team, tenant, workflow, customer, or request metadata where those signals exist.
Surface common waste patterns, including retries, fallbacks, duplicate requests, failed calls, abandoned branches, cache misses, and oversized context.
Produce a ranked report with evidence-backed findings, confidence notes, recommended engineering changes, and a Blackridge fit decision.
Show supporting events, pricing assumptions, attribution coverage, and data-quality gaps alongside each finding.
Current boundaries
The assessment does not guarantee savings or treat modeled opportunity as realized waste.
Missing attribution, sampling, incomplete traces, and provider export gaps are reported explicitly.
Ongoing Blackridge deployment is scoped separately after the evidence and fit are reviewed.
Automated routing, caching, enforcement, or governance is not implied unless explicitly included in an agreed deployment scope.
