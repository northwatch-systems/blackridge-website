The Northwatch Papers

What We Publish, and What We Don't

Arguments belong in public. Build plans don't. The rule we follow, in one page.

The Northwatch Papers argue for disciplines: forensic integrity, economic forensics, behavioral governance. Arguments belong in public. We publish the definitions, the vocabulary, the failure modes, and the reasoning, and we intend for other teams to use them — including teams that never become customers.

The engineering that implements those disciplines is a different kind of document. The full papers contain design requirements, data structures, capture architectures, and worked construction detail. Publishing that material would not build trust; it would hand out a build plan while telling you nothing extra about whether the thinking is sound. The briefs on this site carry the complete argument of each paper. The gated material is the how, never a hidden version of the what.

So the rule we follow:

  • Public: what each discipline is, why existing tools don’t provide it, what a system must be able to answer, what goes wrong without it, and the vocabulary for talking about all of that.
  • Gated: how our implementation works — structures, architectures, detection methods, scoring. Available to customers and teams evaluating Blackridge, under NDA, in working sessions where questions go both ways. The core mechanisms are patent pending.
  • Nowhere: anything derived from customer engagements. Numbers in our public material are synthetic or explicitly rescaled examples, and are labeled as such.

The same honesty rules that govern our reports govern these pages. Observed results and modeled opportunities are never blended into one number. Unknown values are reported as unknown, never silently zeroed. Where a public page summarizes a gated paper, the page says so.

If you want the full papers, ask. We share them with teams we work with and teams seriously evaluating whether to — that conversation starts at a 20-minute call or through the assessment request.
