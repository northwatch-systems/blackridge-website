Where the money goes

Your inference bill is growing faster than your visibility into it.

Most teams running production AI can't answer basic operational questions: which team is spending what, how much traffic is duplicate, how often a retry turns one request into five, how much premium-model usage is actually necessary. The spend is real and rising. The attribution isn't there. Here is where it leaks.

LEAK 01

Zero attribution

“The bill went up and no one can tell me which team or feature drove it.” Cost exists; ownership doesn't.

LEAK 02

Premium-model overuse

The most expensive model is the default for everything — including requests a smaller model would answer identically.

LEAK 03

Duplicate inference

Identical and near-identical requests are paid for again and again, because nothing caches them at the runtime.

LEAK 04

Retry amplification

One user request becomes three to five billed calls under naive retry logic — and never shows up cleanly on the bill.

LEAK 05

Routing inefficiency

Traffic is pinned to one provider by hardcoded clients, so you can't shift load on cost, latency, or availability.

THE META-PROBLEM
