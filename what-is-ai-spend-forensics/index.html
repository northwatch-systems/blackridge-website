Category definition

What is AI Runtime Economics?

AI Runtime Economics is the discipline of knowing, with evidence, what every AI dollar bought at runtime: which workflow spent it, which team owns it, what changed, what was wasted, and what the fix is worth.

Cloud spend got FinOps. AI spend — probabilistic, retry-amplified, agent-fanned — needs a stronger standard: attribution and economic forensics at the request level, reconciled against the invoice.

Common waste classes

Retry amplification

One action fans out into repeated model calls.

Fallback tax

Failure paths route to more expensive models or providers.

Duplicate inference

Similar work is paid for repeatedly.

Unattributed spend

Costs cannot be assigned to a team, app, tenant, or workflow.

Premium-model overuse

Expensive models become defaults for work that may not require them.

Abandoned branches

Agent branches spend tokens without affecting the final answer.

The evidence standard

Findings separate observed waste — tied to real events — from modeled opportunity, a labeled estimate engineering should validate before treating it as savings. Every finding carries a confidence level and a coverage note: attribution is only as strong as the available metadata, and gaps are reported instead of hidden behind a chart.

How it differs from monitoring and FinOps

Observability and tracing tools are useful for debugging, latency, errors, and quality — they answer whether your systems are healthy. FinOps and cloud cost platforms allocate totals — they answer what the invoice says. AI Runtime Economics answers a third question neither is built for: which workflow, team, and decision the money actually bought, with evidence rows that survive a finance review.

How Blackridge fits

Blackridge, from Northwatch Systems, is the first platform built for this discipline: a near real-time attribution and forensics engine for LLM and agent workflows. A spend review can start with only your work email and monthly AI spend bracket; a founder will ask sharper questions from there.

The purpose is proof before platform commitment: find where spend is leaking, inspect the rows behind the findings, and decide whether ongoing Blackridge deployment is justified.
