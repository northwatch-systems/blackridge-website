How Blackridge explains AI spend

Provider bills show totals. Blackridge shows causes. Every finding carries evidence rows, confidence, and coverage so engineering and finance can inspect why it was made.

The question

Not how much — but why.

Provider dashboards show invoices, aggregate tokens, and model-level usage. Blackridge answers a different question: which workflow drove spend, which runtime behavior caused it, who owns it, what looks wasted, and what to fix first.

Evidence standard

Findings carry proof, not charts.

Each finding includes the supporting context needed to review it. Weak or missing data is visible in the evidence instead of hidden behind a visualization.

01

Observed vs modeled

Observed waste is tied to real events. Modeled opportunity is an estimate — clearly labeled — that engineering should validate before treating it as savings.

02

Coverage and confidence

Attribution is only as strong as the available metadata. Blackridge reports gaps so teams know when a finding is well-supported, inferred, or needs better instrumentation.

03

No hidden assumptions

Affected workflow, request references, model and pricing assumptions, ownership metadata, and coverage notes — all visible. Nothing behind a black box.

Boundaries

What Blackridge does not claim.

Blackridge does not guarantee savings, replace engineering judgment, or claim perfect attribution when metadata is incomplete. It gives leaders structured evidence they can share with finance, executives, and application teams.
