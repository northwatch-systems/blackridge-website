FAQ

Questions security, platform, and finance ask first.

Short answers for the teams deciding whether Blackridge belongs in the AI request path.

Value

What does Blackridge actually tell me?

Why the bill moved, which workflow spent it, who owns it, what changed, what was wasted, what proves the finding, and what the fix is worth.

Workflow Owner Waste Evidence Fix value
Evidence

Can finance trust the numbers?

Yes. Observed waste and modeled opportunity are never blended, every claim carries confidence and coverage, and engineering can re-run the investigation from the request ledger.

Value

Is this FinOps tooling or observability?

It's the evidence layer both are missing for AI. Observability tells you systems are healthy. FinOps allocates the invoice. Blackridge proves what the spend bought.

Deployment

Does Blackridge sit in my request path?

Yes. Inline position makes numbers counted instead of sampled from logs, and makes policy enforcement possible later without re-architecture. Start with one workflow.

Deployment

What happens if Blackridge goes down?

Traffic proceeds directly to your provider. Blackridge is fail-open, with a documented bypass you control.

Security

Do you see our prompts and outputs?

Attribution runs on metadata. No payload retention by default. Details are on the security page.

Deployment

Which providers do you support?

OpenAI and Anthropic are the primary design-partner surfaces. AWS Bedrock, Azure OpenAI, Google Gemini, and self-hosted models are scoped per deployment.

Evidence

What comes in a cause file?

Workflow, owner, cost driver, observed waste, modeled opportunity, confidence, coverage, evidence rows, and a recommended first test.

Security

Can security review the architecture before deployment?

Yes. We expect platform and security review before production traffic, and a founding engineer will sit in that review.

Engagement

How fast do we see value?

Design partners get a cause file within 30 days. Most leaks show up in the first week of traffic.

Engagement

Who am I actually talking to?

A founding engineer. We're a small, senior team of principal-level infrastructure engineers; there is no sales floor.
