Tool by tool

Five adjacent tools. One gap they all share.

None of them are wrong for what they do. The gap is that none of them reconstruct expected vs actual workflow cost and explain where every dollar went, with supporting evidence.

Provider dashboards
OpenAI · Anthropic · Gemini consoles

Per-provider, after-the-fact reporting. No cross-provider view, no internal attribution — you see what you spent with that provider, not which application, team, or workflow caused it.

Blackridge reconstructs spend across providers and attributes it to teams and workflows with supporting evidence.

Observes one provider, after the fact
Generic API gateways
Kong · Apigee · Envoy

Route bytes and enforce HTTP policies. They have no concept of model pricing, workflow-level cost, or which requests relate to each other — a request is a request, not an inference call with a cost per token.

Blackridge reasons in workflow economics, not just HTTP. Cost reconstruction, pattern detection, and configurable spend controls are model- and token-aware.

Routes bytes, not economics
Cloud cost & FinOps tools
Generic cost platforms

Excellent at aggregating and reporting cloud spend across services. They work from billing exports — which means they see cost after it's incurred and can't change it.

Blackridge reconstructs why the cost happened and can use runtime interception for production evidence quality before teams add controls.

Reports cost, can't change it
LLM routers & OSS proxies
Open-source gateways

Often built as developer-convenience proxies — useful for switching providers during experimentation, but not built for enterprise-grade spend forensics: limited workflow attribution, coverage reporting, or supporting evidence.

Blackridge is Kubernetes-native, observable, air-gap capable, and built to be owned by platform teams — not just used by individual developers.

Dev convenience, not platform infra
Agentic AI platforms
Orchestration suites

Solve a different problem at a different layer — how to orchestrate multi-step AI workflows. They generate inference traffic; they don't govern it.

Blackridge is infrastructure beneath them. Every inference call an orchestration layer makes becomes auditable spend evidence.

Generates traffic, doesn't govern it
